License Raj

Back in the 90s and before that, India was a closeted economy and companies had to jump loops and get permission for almost everything under the sun from the governement.

You know you have made it in India, when a tax or a regulation comes after you and your business.

This section looks at the different announcements by the governments (states and central) and nationalised companies to ride the startup PR.

  • DIPP received applications from 400 companies and now it is in the hands of babus and their counterparts to decide whether these are truly innovative or not.
  • State Bank of India, still reeling under the pressure of Rs 98,000 Crore of bad loans launches a Rs 200 Cr fund for fintech startups, a week after banning the option to load money into wallets via netbanking. Hmm!
  • HE Finance Minister, Mr Arun Jaitley has proclaimed that GST will likely be applied on all online purchases from April next year.
  • Indian Patent Office issues guidelines to startups, which reads
a startup willing to file a patent application for an invention will have to select a facilitator who would help in preparing the request and also assess the patentability of the invention as per acts and rules, the Controller General Patents, Designs and Trade Marks said in a public notice.

because when you can take our a regulation, why not.

  • Govt looks to fix an upper-limit to fares of cab-aggregators like Ola and Uber. How can companies come and earn right from under us. And that too, honestly! is what they must be thinking. raises fists.
  • Handsets in India must have support for typing in English, Hindi and one more regional language, says BIS.

Mergers and Acquisitions

Here are all the mergers and acquisitions in the last week.
